Yahtzee scoring and strategy

Yahtzee rewards patience as much as luck. Once you understand how the scoring works, a few habits will lift your typical game by fifty points or more. Here is how the numbers add up and where the smart plays are.

Secure the 35-point bonus

The upper bonus is the single biggest swing you can plan for. You need 63 across Ones to Sixes, which works out to three of each number on average. The maths is forgiving: three of a high number buys room to fall short on a low one. Three sixes and three fives alone are worth 33 of the 63, so chase the big numbers first and treat the bonus as a target you are steadily banking, not an afterthought.

Keep the Yahtzee box open

The first Yahtzee scores 50, and every extra one is worth a 100-point bonus. Those bonuses are the difference between a good game and a great one, so avoid scratching the Yahtzee box early. If you are stuck for somewhere to put an awkward roll, there is almost always a cheaper box to give up.

Know when to scratch

You cannot win every box, so losing one cheaply is a skill. A few rules of thumb:

  • Sacrifice Ones before a bigger box. A scratched Ones costs you at most five points; a scratched Sixes or Large Straight costs far more.
  • The straights are the hardest boxes to fill late, so fill or scratch them while you still have rolls to spare.
  • Chance is your safety valve. Keep it for a turn where the dice give you nothing else, and it will soak up a high roll for free.

Play the odds

Three of a Kind and Four of a Kind score the total of all five dice, so they reward high numbers, not just matches. When you have three of a kind and two rolls left, it is often worth chasing the fourth die rather than settling. Small and Large Straights, by contrast, pay a fixed 30 and 40, so once you have them, take them.
