How to play Yahtzee

Yahtzee is a dice game for one or more players. Over thirteen turns each you roll five dice, chase combinations and fill in a score sheet. The highest total at the end wins. Here is everything you need to start playing.

What you need

Five dice, a cup for shaking if you like, and a score sheet for each game. You can keep score with pen and paper or use this free online Yahtzee score sheet, which offers only the legal scores for each box so nobody has to do the arithmetic.

How a turn works

On your turn you get up to three rolls:

  1. Roll all five dice.
  2. Set aside any dice you want to keep, then re-roll the rest. You may do this twice, so three rolls in total.
  3. After your last roll you must choose one unused category and write your score in that box. You only fill one box per turn.

You do not have to use all three rolls. If your first roll already gives you a combination you are happy with, you can stop and score it straight away.

The thirteen categories

The score sheet has two halves. The upper section is about collecting a single number; the lower section rewards particular combinations.

The Yahtzee bonus

Roll a Yahtzee, score it for 50, and every further Yahtzee you roll in the same game earns a 100-point bonus on top. This is where big scores come from, so it pays to keep the Yahtzee box open when you can.

Scratching a box

If your dice do not fit any category you still want, you must scratch one: enter a zero in an unused box to give it up. Choosing which box to sacrifice is one of the real decisions in the game.

Winning

After thirteen turns every box is filled. Add the upper section and its bonus, the lower section and any Yahtzee bonuses. The player with the highest grand total wins. Ready to play? Start scoring a game now, or read our Yahtzee scoring and strategy guide to sharpen your play.
